NPRG stands for "Network Programmable Robot Game". Although it can hardly be called a game in the ordinary sense. It's more of an intellectual entertaiment like crosswords. :)

Here is the point of the game. You have a robot in an unexplored labyrinth. There also are your opponents' robots there. The player wins if his robot is first at the finish (in the "Labyrinth" gamemode), or if his robot is the last to stay alive (in the "Kill`em all!" gamemode).

The feature of the game is that you cannot control your robot directly using keyboard or mouse. You assign it's logic and it acts following your instructions.

For more information about this application, about creating a new game or joining an existing game, you may refer to "What is NPRG?" section.

To find out about different gamemodes and how to control your robot see... "Controlling your robot".

Detailed descriptions of all the language functions you can use to create the robot logic you'll find in the "Language overview".
